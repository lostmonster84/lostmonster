## Language Guidelines

### Words to USE

**Business/Results Language:**
- Revenue, profit, growth
- Time saved, cost reduced
- Bookings, conversions, sales
- Automated, systematic, efficient
- ROI, payback period

**Honest/Direct Language:**
- Built, fixed, delivered
- No BS, straightforward, direct
- Real, proven, tested
- Fair pricing, transparent
- Guaranteed, promise

**Modern/Efficient Language:**
- Modern tools (not "AI-powered")
- Fast development, quick turnaround
- Efficient, streamlined, systematic
- Custom-built, tailored, fitted

**Credibility Language:**
- Business owner perspective
- Lived experience
- Track record, proven results
- Client testimonials, case studies

### Words to AVOID

**Buzzwords/Jargon:**
- ❌ "AI-powered" (sounds cheap/gimmicky)
- ❌ "Innovative solutions" (meaningless)
- ❌ "Cutting-edge technology" (vague)
- ❌ "Synergy" (corporate BS)
- ❌ "Leverage" (overused)
- ❌ "Ecosystem" (pretentious)
- ❌ "Disruptive" (tired)

**Vague Promises:**
- ❌ "We build amazing things"
- ❌ "Exceed expectations"
- ❌ "World-class quality"
- ❌ "Best-in-class"
- ❌ "Unparalleled service"

**Corporate Speak:**
- ❌ "Utilize" (use "use")
- ❌ "Solution provider"
- ❌ "Stakeholder engagement"
- ❌ "Strategic partnership"
- ❌ "Going forward"

**Technical Jargon (unless explaining):**
- ❌ Don't lead with: "Isomorphic React apps with Next.js"
- ✅ Do say: "Fast, modern web applications"
- ❌ Don't lead with: "Postgres-backed REST APIs"
- ✅ Do say: "Reliable database systems"

## Tone of Voice

### Characteristics

**1. Confident but not arrogant**
- ✅ "I've built 50+ systems. I know what works."
- ❌ "I'm the best developer you'll ever meet."

**2. Direct but not harsh**
- ✅ "That approach won't work for your business. Here's why..."
- ❌ "That's a stupid idea."

**3. Professional but not corporate**
- ✅ "Let's hop on a call to discuss your booking system."
- ❌ "We would be delighted to schedule a strategic consultation regarding your reservation management infrastructure."

**4. Technical but not jargony**
- ✅ "I'll build you a custom booking system with automated confirmations."
- ❌ "I'll architect a Node.js microservice with async email queuing via Bull/Redis."

**5. Honest but not brutal**
- ✅ "A full rebuild will cost £8k. Cheaper to start fresh than fix the mess."
- ❌ "Your current developer is incompetent and this code is garbage."

### Voice Examples

**Good Examples:**

"I run multiple businesses myself. When I see expensive software subscriptions, I build custom alternatives. Same for my clients—I build what you need, you own it forever, no monthly fees."

"Your booking system is costing you money. Double bookings, manual processes, expensive channel managers. I've fixed this for 50+ properties. 2-4 weeks, fixed price, done properly."

"I don't pitch you features you don't need. I listen to your business problems, build what fixes them, deliver on time. That's it."

**Bad Examples:**

❌ "Our innovative platform leverages cutting-edge AI to deliver world-class solutions."

❌ "We're a full-service digital transformation consultancy specializing in enterprise-grade implementations."

❌ "Lost Monster is proud to offer best-in-class development services utilizing the latest frameworks."

## Common Objections & Responses

### "How can you be 70% cheaper than agencies?"

**Response:**
"Agencies have big overheads—offices, account managers, sales teams. I use modern development tools that let me build faster, and I work directly with you—no middlemen. You get the same quality for a fraction of the cost."

### "If you're cheap, won't quality suffer?"

**Response:**
"I'm not cheap, I'm fairly priced. Check my reviews—4.9/5 average, 100% on-time delivery. I stake my business reputation on every project. Fast doesn't mean sloppy when you use the right tools."

### "Can you handle my project if you're solo?"

**Response:**
"Being solo is an advantage for most projects. Direct communication, no telephone game, faster decisions. For your booking system, it's me building it start to finish. If I genuinely can't handle it, I'll tell you upfront."

### "Why should I trust someone remote/international?"

**Response:**
"Location doesn't matter when you deliver results. I've worked with 50+ clients, mostly in the UK. Clear communication, regular updates, all contracts in place. Check my reviews and case studies—results speak louder than geography."

### "What if you disappear after building it?"

**Response:**
"I don't do one-off projects and disappear. Average client relationship is 12+ months. I want you to succeed because that's how I get referrals. I provide ongoing support, even after launch."

### "Can you build everything I need?"

**Response:**
"I'm honest about what I can and can't do. Booking systems, e-commerce, custom business apps—yes. If you need a 100-person enterprise system, I'll tell you to find an agency. I focus on what I'm great at."

**Formula:**

**Structure:**
1. Client & Industry
2. Problem they had
3. What you built
4. Results (with numbers)
5. Client quote

**Focus on metrics:**
- Revenue increase (%)
- Time saved (hours/week)
- Cost reduction (£/month)
- Conversion improvement (%)
- Bookings increase (%)

### Testimonials

**Ask for:**
- Specific results (numbers)
- What problem you solved
- What it was like to work with you
- Would they recommend (and to who)

**Good testimonial:**
"Our bookings tripled in the first month. The system just works, and Lost Monster made the whole process easy to understand. Saved us 10 hours a week in manual work." - Sarah MacLeod, Highland Spa

**Bad testimonial:**
"Great developer, highly recommended!" - Anonymous

## Dos and Don'ts Summary

### DO

✅ Speak in first person when appropriate
✅ Use real numbers and metrics
✅ Be honest about what you can/can't do
✅ Focus on business outcomes
✅ Use client testimonials with specifics
✅ Mention hospitality focus when relevant
✅ Be direct and clear
✅ Explain technical concepts simply

### DON'T

❌ Use "we" if you're solo (be honest)
❌ Make vague promises
❌ Use buzzwords (AI-powered, innovative, etc.)
❌ Pretend to be bigger than you are
❌ Lead with technical jargon
❌ Copy competitor messaging
❌ Fake Scottish identity
❌ Oversell or hype

## Message Testing

### Before Publishing Any Message, Ask:

1. **Is it true?** (No exaggeration or misleading claims)
2. **Is it clear?** (Could a 12-year-old understand it?)
3. **Is it specific?** (Does it say something concrete or just fluff?)
4. **Is it you?** (Does it sound like how you actually talk?)
5. **Is it helpful?** (Does it answer a question or solve a problem?)

If yes to all 5 → publish
If no to any → rewrite

---

**When in doubt, be more honest and more direct. That's the Lost Monster way.**
