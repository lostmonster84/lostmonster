## The Name

**Lost Monster = Loch Ness Monster**

The name comes from the Loch Ness Monster, a legendary creature from Scottish folklore. "Lost" because it's elusive, mysterious, rarely seen—but when it appears, it's memorable.

### Why This Name Works

**For the Brand:**
- Memorable (not generic "Web Solutions Ltd")
- Story-driven (has meaning)
- Scottish connection (but not limiting)
- Playful without being unprofessional
- Unique in the development space

**The Metaphor:**
- Elusive/rare (quality is hard to find)
- Legendary (builds legendary systems)
- Mysterious (not showy, quietly effective)
- Scottish roots (original client base)

## The Real Beginning

### Where It Started

**Location:** Scottish Highlands (Loch Ness region)
**First Clients:** Scottish businesses (hotels, accommodations, local services)
**Genesis:** Built systems for Scottish properties struggling with booking chaos

### The Initial Problem

Scottish hospitality businesses were paying ridiculous amounts for:
- Channel managers (£500+/month)
- Booking systems that didn't fit their needs
- Generic solutions built for enterprise, not small properties
- Systems that created more work than they solved

**The Solution:**
Custom booking systems built specifically for each property. Fixed price. They own it forever. No monthly fees unless they want ongoing support.

### Early Success

**Ancarraig Highland Cottages** - First major success
- Custom booking system
- Eliminated double bookings
- 24/7 automated bookings
- Replaced expensive channel manager
- Property owner thrilled, referred others

**Word Spread:**
Scottish hospitality is a tight-knit community. One happy client led to referrals. "Lost Monster" became known for fixing booking chaos.

## The Lost Monster Philosophy

### Core Beliefs

**1. Business Understanding > Technical Skills**

Technical skills are table stakes. What clients really need is someone who understands their business problems. I have both.

**2. Honest Over Impressive**

I'd rather tell you the truth than win the project with BS. If there's a cheaper solution, I'll recommend it. Honesty builds long-term relationships.

**3. Results Over Features**

I don't care about building cool features. I care about business outcomes. Does it increase revenue? Save time? Reduce costs? That's what matters.

**4. Fair Pricing Over Maximum Extraction**

I could charge more. But I remember being a cash-strapped business owner. Fair pricing means repeat clients and referrals. That's better long-term.

**5. Systematic Over Heroic**

I don't do "heroic" all-nighters to save projects. I do systematic development that prevents problems. Boring and reliable beats exciting and unpredictable.
